World Shark Attacks

1950 to Present


Project Overview

The purpose of this project was to look at records of world shark attacks and the various factors that play into that including location, time of day, activity being done at the time of attack, gender, etc. From that information we then built a machine-learning model that could be used to predict the liklihood of a shark attack being fatal.

The dataset we used was from the Global Shark Attack File maintained by the Shark Research Institute. We narrowed our dataset down to the years between 1950 and current. We did this in order to have situations that were more relatable to our current time by eliminating major war years that included a large number of sea disasters.

We used Tableau for our visualizations. We used Logistic Regression and Neural Network models in order to build models that could be used to predict fatal versus non-fatal shark attacks given certain factors.
